What We Repair

Cool Room & Freezer Room Faults We Fix

From compressor to control panel, here's what we diagnose and repair most for Sydney commercial cool rooms and freezer rooms.

Compressor Failures

Compressor not starting, cycling excessively, or tripping out? We diagnose and repair compressor faults on-site.

Evaporator & Condenser Faults

Iced-up evaporators, blocked condensers or fan failures causing poor airflow and warm rooms.

Door Seals & Hinges

Damaged seals, hinges or latches let cold air escape and strain your system. Fast repair or replacement.

Panel & Insulation Damage

Damaged or de-laminated panels affecting insulation and temperature stability. Repaired or replaced on-site.

Refrigerant Leaks & Re-Gas

Refrigerant leaks causing poor performance or no cooling. Leak detection, repair and re-gas by licensed technicians.

Electrical & Control Panel Faults

Faulty controllers, sensors or wiring causing erratic temperatures or unexpected shutdowns.

Temperature Fluctuation Diagnostics

Inconsistent temperatures putting stock at risk? A full diagnostic finds the root cause, not just the symptom.

Emergency Stock-Loss Prevention

Sudden breakdown? We prioritise emergency callouts where food safety and stock are at risk.

Common Questions

Cool Room & Freezer Room Repair FAQs

How fast does a cool room or freezer room actually need repairing?

It depends on stock and conditions, but most commercial cool rooms and freezer rooms should be repaired within hours, not days, once temperature starts rising. We treat these as priority callouts.

Can you repair refrigerant leaks?

Yes. Our licensed technicians can detect, repair and re-gas refrigerant leaks in line with relevant handling regulations.

Do you repair both cool rooms and freezer rooms?

Yes — we repair both cool room and freezer room refrigeration systems, panels, doors and controls.

What's usually the cause of a cool room running warm?

Common causes include compressor failure, blocked condensers, damaged door seals, refrigerant leaks or faulty controllers. An on-site diagnostic identifies the actual cause.
